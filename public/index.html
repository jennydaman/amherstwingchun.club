Q&A

What is Wing Chun?
Wing chun 咏春 is a Chinese martial art which emphasizes simplicity and technique over raw strength.
According to legend… A new style of kung fu was developed by a Shaolin nun who taught it to another woman in secret. That woman used the art to win a duel against thugs, saving her from being forced into an unwanted marriage. Her name was Wing Chun. The literal translation of the Chinese characters “咏春” means “singing spring.” True or not, this tale depicts how the wing chun style of kung fu is about how the physically weak might defend itself against the strong.
Do I need to be athletic?
No. Wing chun focuses on simple and direct movements, unlike most other kung fu styles and martial arts. In fact, wing chun is often practiced in China by senior communities similarly to Tai Chi.
How much do lessons cost?
I hope to make the lessons free—cultural knowledge should be freely accessible to all. If we have to pay money to rent a space, I will set tuition at something between $5–10/week. Whatever money left over will be donated to LUCE.
