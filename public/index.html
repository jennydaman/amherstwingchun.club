Q&A

What is Wing Chun?
Wing chun 咏春 is a Chinese martial art which emphasizes simplicity and technique over raw strength.
According to legend

A new style of kung fu was developed by a refugee nun who taught it to another woman in secret. That woman used the art to win a duel against thugs, saving her from being forced into an unwanted marriage. Her name was Wing Chun. The literal translation of the Chinese characters “咏春” means “singing spring.” True or not, this tale depicts how the wing chun style of kung fu is about how the physically weak might defend itself against the strong.

In real life, Wing Chun was made famous internationally first by Bruce Lee, then again by Donnie Yen in his Ip Man series of movies.

Do I need to be athletic?

No. Wing chun focuses on simple and direct movements, unlike most other kung fu styles and martial arts. In fact, wing chun theory is similar to that of Tai Chi, which you may know of as a common form of exercise for senior communities.

What is kung fu class like?
Close-distance partner drills with light contact

Focusing on repetitive partner drills is a modern approach to practical martial arts instruction. This is different from traditional teaching styles. We hope to provide an alternative to learning martial arts that is less intimidating and for busy people. For reference, traditional kung fu and karate lessons typically involve 30 minutes of stretching, calisthenics, and meditation, followed by rehearsal of forms (kata, 套路), which are choreographed sequences of fighting postures.

Is kung fu practical for self-defense?

tl;dr no. We'll talk about it a bit but teaching self-defense is not an objective of the Amherst Wing Chun Club. Our objectives are to have fun, appreciate Chinese culture, and make friends. If you have any expectations outside of that list then please do not come.

No martial arts, neither traditional nor MMA, is practical for self-defense. No martial artist will tell you otherwise unless they are running a for-profit business and are trying to get customers. If you want to learn self-defense then look for a self-defense class not martial arts. The most important self-defense skill is situational awareness. This world is full of lunatics and here in the U.S. most of them have guns. Get real.
